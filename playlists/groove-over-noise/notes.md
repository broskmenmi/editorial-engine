# GROOVE OVER NOISE — Editorial Notes

## Structural principles

- Sequence for a long-form listening journey, not a live DJ set.
- Do not require beatmatching, but require bodily pulse continuity.
- Target adjacent BPM differences of 0–4 BPM.
- Flag 5–7 BPM differences for direct listening evidence.
- Reject differences above 7 BPM unless half-time/double-time equivalence or an intentional reset is documented.
- Apply the strictest standard to the opening three tracks.
- Avoid accidental tempo sawtoothing; deliberate wave motion must be supported by chapter roles and perceived pressure.
- Use concrete track anchors plus structural roles when inserting tracks.
- Prefer continuity over isolated highlights.
- Increase density before intensity.
- Let pressure accumulate through progression rather than drops.
- Preserve decompression space near the end.
- Spotify Mix cannot repair or validate a defective transition.
- User listening feedback is direct audit evidence.

## Long-form storytelling model

The playlist should grow as **multiple waves with one dominant summit**:

1. **Arrival** — space, patience, low information density.
2. **Groove formation** — establish trust and sustained bodily movement.
3. **Local crest** — prove physical weight without spending the main summit.
4. **Partial release** — reduce pressure without returning to zero.
5. **Re-entry** — begin a new chapter from a stronger baseline.
6. **Deeper crest** — reveal a new texture, rhythm, or form of pressure.
7. **Partial release** — create contrast while preserving immersion.
8. **Main summit** — the strongest sustained physical and emotional concentration.
9. **Long decompression** — move from force to repetition, texture, and space.
10. **Dissolution** — close without reopening the journey.

Story rules:
- Several local crests are allowed; only one summit should dominate the complete journey.
- Later crests must reveal something new, not repeat the same build louder.
- Partial releases must preserve accumulated immersion.
- Re-entry must not sound like a fresh playlist start.
- The main summit is defined by combined pressure, density, duration, and attention demand, not BPM alone.
- New chapters should normally be inserted before the final decompression and dissolution.
- Do not append new material after the established closer unless the ending itself is being replaced.
- Avoid repeating the same build-drop shape in consecutive chapters.

## Duration scaling

- **Under 60 minutes:** one complete wave may be sufficient.
- **60–120 minutes:** normally develop at least two waves.
- **Two to three hours:** normally develop three or four chapters with local crests.
- **Beyond three hours:** use several chapters, one dominant summit, and a substantial final decompression.

These are editorial guides rather than mechanical quotas.
