# GROOVE OVER NOISE — Constitution

This playlist is not built around genres.

It is built around behaviour.

Every addition should strengthen a single philosophy:

**Groove is more valuable than noise.**

Groove is sustained movement.

Noise is anything that interrupts it.

When selecting music, prefer rhythm over impact.

Prefer progression over spectacle.

Prefer tension over intensity.

Prefer texture over melody.

Prefer immersion over attention.

Prefer patience over immediacy.

Prefer timelessness over novelty.

The playlist should reward listening rather than demand it.

The strongest tracks rarely announce themselves.

They reveal themselves.

Choose music that becomes more compelling over time instead of exhausting its ideas immediately.

The playlist should feel like one continuous journey rather than a collection of individual songs.

Listeners should gradually stop noticing where one record ends and the next begins.

Every transition should feel inevitable.

Every track should leave space for the next.

Space is part of the composition.

Reduction is part of the composition.

Silence is part of the groove.

Artists are reference points, not limitations.

The philosophy matters more than the names.

## Reject tracks that rely primarily on

- spectacle
- obvious hooks
- dramatic breakdowns
- commercial energy
- trend-driven production
- unnecessary complexity
- aggression without purpose
- novelty without longevity

## Accept tracks that

- reward repeated listening
- resident DJs would trust at four in the morning
- strengthen the identity even when unfamiliar
- create continuity instead of isolated moments
- increase immersion without demanding attention

Every addition should make the playlist more recognisable, not merely more varied.

If a track makes people dance because it is exciting, question it.

If a track makes people dance because they forgot they were listening, keep it.

The objective is not to create memorable moments.

The objective is to create unforgettable nights.

When uncertain between two records, choose the one that disappears into the journey rather than the one that interrupts it.

Every track should answer one question:

**Does this choose groove over noise?**

If the answer is anything less than an immediate yes, it does not belong.
